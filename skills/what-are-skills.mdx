## The simple version

A **Skill** is a set of instructions that teaches Claude how to handle specific tasks your way. Instead of re-explaining your preferences, brand guidelines, or workflows every single conversation, you teach Claude once — and it remembers.

Think of it like onboarding a new employee with your playbooks. Except this employee never forgets, never loses the doc, and applies it automatically every time.

## Why skills matter

Without a skill, every conversation with Claude starts from zero. You have to re-explain context, re-share formatting preferences, re-describe your brand colors. It's like having a brilliant colleague with amnesia.

With a skill installed, Claude already knows:

- Your brand guidelines before you ask for a deck
- Your writing tone before you draft an email
- Your industry terminology before you explain a workflow
- Your document structure before you create a report

The result: you go from spending 5 minutes setting context to just saying "make me a deck" and getting something on-brand the first time.

## What's inside a skill?

A skill is just a markdown file called **SKILL.md**. No code, no plugins, no API keys. It's a structured document that tells Claude:

| Section | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **What to do** | The task or domain the skill covers |
| **How to do it** | Specific rules, patterns, templates, and preferences |
| **What to avoid** | Common mistakes or things you don't want |
| **Examples** | What good output looks like |

For example, our [slide generation skill (brand CSS)](/skills/federato-brand) includes the exact hex codes for every brand color, which fonts to use for headlines vs. body text, 10+ slide layout patterns, and a pre-flight checklist Claude runs through before finalizing a presentation.

## How to install a skill

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the skill file">
    Get the .zip containing SKILL.md and any supporting files (e.g. from the skill page in this hub).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Claude desktop app">
    Go to **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Skills section">
    Click **+ Add** and upload the SKILL.md file. If the skill includes a .css file or assets folder, add those too.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done">
    Claude will use the skill automatically in every relevant conversation going forward. You don't re-upload. You don't remind Claude. It just knows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Skills + MCP: the real power

**Skills** tell Claude *how* to do things. **MCP** (Model Context Protocol) gives Claude *access* to your tools — Google Calendar, Gmail, Slack, Granola, and more.

| Alone | Together |
|-------|----------|
| **Skill alone:** "Create a Federato-branded deck" — Claude builds it with the right colors and fonts. | **Skill + MCP:** "Prep me for my UFG call tomorrow" — Claude checks your calendar, pulls context from Granola transcripts, knows relationship dynamics from your account data, and formats everything using your preferred structure. All without you explaining any of it. |
| **MCP alone:** "Check my calendar" — Claude sees your schedule. | This is what turns Claude from a chatbot into an actual workflow tool. |

## What makes a good skill?

The best skills are specific, opinionated, and grounded in real work.

- **Be specific, not generic.** "Use professional formatting" is useless. "Use Frank Ruhl Libre for headlines at 36pt in Meridian Maroon (#2D1D1A)" produces consistent results.
- **Include examples.** Show Claude what good looks like. A single example of a well-formatted Salesforce update is worth more than a paragraph describing one.
- **Cover the edge cases.** What should Claude do when there's not enough data? When the format doesn't fit? The more decisions you make upfront, the less you correct later.
- **Iterate based on feedback.** Your first version won't be perfect. Use the skill, notice where Claude gets it wrong, update the instructions. Skills get better over time.
