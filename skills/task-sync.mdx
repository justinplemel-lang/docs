## Task Sync

Task Sync gives every team member an automated daily recap of their open action items, posted to a personal Slack channel twice a day. It pulls from Gmail, Granola meeting transcripts, and Slack — then tracks items across syncs so completed tasks stop showing up.

The system runs two scheduled syncs on weekdays:

- **Morning (8:00 AM)** — Recaps overnight email, recent meetings, and Slack activity. Shows what's on your plate for the day.
- **End of Day (5:00 PM)** — Captures the full day's activity, marks completed items, and surfaces what carries into tomorrow.

</Info>

Task Sync is included in the [Federato CS Plugin](/plugins/federato-cs). Install the plugin in Cowork and say **"set up my task sync"** to get started. No separate download.

## How completion tracking works

The main improvement over a simple daily digest is that Task Sync remembers what was open yesterday and detects when things get done. It uses three layers:

### Explicit signals

When you finish something, post a quick message in your task channel:

- Reply to a sync message: `done: sent the compliance follow-up`
- Post standalone: `✅ finished the forms audit`
- Copy items from the sync and add ✅ next to them

The next sync reads your messages and clears matching items.

### Implicit detection

Even without you saying anything, the sync picks up on resolution signals:

- You replied to an email that was listed as "respond to X"
- A meeting that needed prep already happened
- A Slack thread went quiet after a final resolution message
- Someone confirmed receipt of something you sent

### Stale item flagging

Items that appear in 3+ consecutive syncs get separated into a "Carried Forward" section with a ⏳ flag, so lingering items are visible and you can decide whether to act or drop them.

## Setup

Setting up Task Sync takes about 2 minutes. In Cowork, say **"set up my task sync"** and Claude walks you through it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your channel">
    Claude creates a personal Slack channel (e.g., `#yourname-tasks`) or connects to one you already have.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what to monitor">
    Tell Claude which Slack channels and DMs to watch for action items — account channels, team channels, and key DMs where tasks come in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your VIPs">
    Name the people whose requests should always get highlighted in a dedicated VIP section (e.g., your manager, key stakeholders).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm schedule">
    Default is 8:00 AM and 5:00 PM weekdays. Adjust if you prefer different times.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your configuration is saved to memory and persists across sessions. The scheduled tasks start automatically the next business day.

## What the recaps look like

Each sync posts a structured message to your channel:

```
☀️ Morning Recap — Monday, March 16

✅ Completed Since Last Sync
• ~Send compliance follow-up to Will Ross~ — marked done by you
• ~Run sheet through Claude prompt and send Alex questions~ — marked done by you

📧 Email Action Items
• Waiting on Samba Safety API keys — awaiting response

📝 Meeting Action Items
• ⚠️ Map new data science fields ASAP — Monday priority
• Set up subjectivity framework with Brian and Emon

💬 Slack Action Items
• ⚠️ Lynn Black: QBR deck 1st draft due March 23rd
• Tommy Brown: Review Submission Triage Enablement doc

⏳ Carried Forward (3+ syncs)
• Clarify tax/fee disclosure format — first appeared 3/13

⚠️ VIP Items (Will Ross / Lynn Black)
• Will Ross: ~Compliance follow-up sent~ ✅
• Lynn Black: QBR review scheduled Friday — start prepping deck
```

Completed items appear at the top so you can see what's cleared. Open items are organized by source. VIP items get their own section regardless of source.

## Tips

- **Mark things done as you go.** A quick `✅ handled the forms audit` in your channel is faster than waiting for the sync to figure it out.
- **Thread replies work best.** Replying directly to a sync message makes it easiest for the next sync to match completions to specific items.
- **Check the Carried Forward section.** If something sits there for a week, it's probably worth either doing or deliberately dropping.
- **VIPs keep you honest.** Requests from your VIP list always surface, even in a busy sync with 20+ items.
